## Learning goals

- Explain why source visibility alone is not enough.
- Summarize the 10 OSD criteria at a practical level.
- Spot common non-open restrictions.

## Core ideas

OSI defines open source through license criteria. In short, a license must allow software to be used, modified, and shared without discriminatory or technology-specific restrictions.

The 10 criteria cover topics such as:

- Free redistribution
- Availability of source code
- Permission for derived works
- Non-discrimination (people, groups, fields of endeavor)
- License portability and neutrality

## Why it matters

The OSD helps teams avoid “source-available but not open” confusion. It gives a shared baseline for legal and community expectations.

## Practice

Take one license your team uses and map it against the 10 OSD criteria. Mark any areas you are unsure about.
