## Learning goals

- Identify what copyright protects and what rights it grants.
- Explain the role of the public domain.
- Recognize when exceptions and limitations may apply.

## Core ideas

Copyright grants creators exclusive rights (such as reproduction and adaptation) for a limited period. After that term, works enter the **public domain**, where reuse does not require permission.

Not all reuse requires a license. Depending on jurisdiction, legal exceptions and limitations (for example fair dealing/fair use) may allow specific uses.

## Why it matters

CC licenses are built on top of copyright law. You can only license rights you control, and users still keep rights they already have under exceptions and public domain rules.

## Practice

For one work you use often (article, image, video), identify:

- Who likely holds rights
- Whether it may already be public domain
- Whether a legal exception could apply to your use
