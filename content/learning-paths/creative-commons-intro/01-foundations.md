## Learning goals

- Explain why Creative Commons was created.
- Distinguish CC licenses from the CC organization and wider open movement.
- Describe how digital sharing and copyright can be in tension.

## Core ideas

Creative Commons started as a practical response to a problem: the internet made sharing easy, while default copyright made reuse hard. CC licenses give creators a standardized way to grant permissions in advance.

Today, Creative Commons can be viewed in three layers:
- **Legal tools**: licenses and public domain tools.
- **Organization**: the nonprofit stewarding those tools.
- **Movement**: global communities advancing openness in culture, education, and research.

## Why it matters

Without clear reuse permissions, people hesitate to share or remix. CC reduces this friction while keeping attribution and creator intent visible.
